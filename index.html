About Me

As a dedicated researcher with a robust background in simulation, system modeling, design optimization, and data-driven methods, I develop innovative solutions for challenges in fluid mechanics, combustion systems, HVAC thermal loads, and aerospace design. My work spans CFD simulations, optimization workflows, and experimental validation to drive efficiency and insight.

Research Highlights

System-level simulation results: I worked on control-oriented powertrain modeling of an electric school bus for thermal load characterization. The model includes an HVAC-integrated cabin to simulate window and door operations and normal bus activity, assessing their impact on air conditioning load and state-of-charge prediction over a drive cycle.

CFD mid-plane views: Velocity magnitude and pressure contours from thermal airflow analysis of a race car battery pack.

CFD of cordwood-fueled room heater: Temperature distribution and flow patterns from numerical combustion modeling, including catalytic combustion. Provided simulation-driven design modifications to improve efficiency and reduce CO emissions.

Vertical wind tunnel design: Stable droplet levitation model via data-driven optimization. This work is in progress for journal publication, proposing a novel CFD-based design optimization approach.
